Hi, my name is Amanda Klapstein and I have some advice for you!

Stay the course!

Learning to code is inevitably going to get weird and uncomfortable and hard at some point in your journey. Dig in and stay determined. Many students have come before you who are now well into their professional careers.

Everything in this life worth doing takes effort. Some things will come easily and at other times you will struggle. It's also easy to get intimidated by the vast depth of the programming and tech field. Just take it one step at a time. Believe in yourself. Do the work. Before you know it you will be at the top of the mountain.

Practice, practice, practice

Read and write the coding languarge you are learning as much as possible. Throw yourself into the process. I am one those people who learn best by doing. I could watch lectures and read examples all day, but until I actually dive into the project work nothing really sticks...it's all in the ether.

I suggest brainstorming a passion project. Not only will you get experience - you will build your portfolio. Passion projects are awesome for motivation because you're learning your technical skills whilst applying it to a topic or hobby of personal interested. Perfect.

Best of luck!
